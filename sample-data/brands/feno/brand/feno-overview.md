# Feno Company Overview

## Company Mission
After seeing the direct link between oral health and diseases like cancer and heart disease, Feno's founder couldn't stand by while otherwise healthy people developed life-threatening conditions in part because of their poor oral health. Feno's mission is to equip every home with advanced oral health technology that transforms daily routines into comprehensive health monitoring systems, helping people live better and longer by preventing systemic diseases before they start.

## The Problem Feno Solves

### Poor Oral Health Leading to Systemic Disease
- **Key statistics and data points:** 90% of individuals suffer from some form of gum disease; poor oral health is directly linked to heart disease, diabetes, and cancer
- **Specific pain points addressed:**
  - Gum disease prevalence and lack of early detection
  - Oral health complications leading to heart disease, diabetes, and cancer
  - Inability to monitor oral health at home between dental visits
  - Lack of connection between daily oral care and overall health outcomes
- **Target audience struggles:** Health-conscious individuals want to optimize wellness but lack tools to monitor oral health, understand its connection to systemic health, or catch problems early before they require expensive dental interventions

### Time Scarcity and Inconsistent Technique
- **Additional problems solved:** Traditional brushing requires 2 minutes twice daily (21 hours per year) and relies on proper technique, which most people don't execute consistently. Feno reduces brushing time to 20 seconds while eliminating human error through full-mouth simultaneous coverage.

## Market Landscape

### Target Audience
- **Primary:** Health-conscious professionals (25-55 years old, $75k+ household income) who track wellness metrics, invest in preventative health, value time efficiency, and are early adopters of health technology
- **Secondary:** People with dexterity issues (elderly, arthritis), dental-phobic individuals seeking to improve oral health at home, busy parents wanting thorough cleaning for children
- **Decision Makers:** Individual consumers making personal health investments; may also appeal to wellness-focused households making family health decisions

### Current Market Gap
Feno fills the gap between traditional electric toothbrushes (time-consuming, technique-dependent, no health monitoring) and basic U-shaped brushes (lack quality and monitoring features). Feno is the only toothbrush that combines time-efficient full-mouth cleaning with AI-powered health monitoring, creating a new subcategory: "health monitoring smartbrush" rather than just "better toothbrush."

### Product Description
The Feno Smartbrush is a revolutionary oral care system that combines a U-shaped mouthpiece with 18,000 precision nylon bristles for simultaneous full-mouth cleaning in 20 seconds, plus a built-in AI-powered oral scanner that monitors teeth, gums, tongue, and soft tissue to provide personalized health insights and early disease detection.

### Key Features
1. **20-Second Full-Mouth Clean:** U-shaped mouthpiece with 18,000 nylon bristles (7x more than standard brushes) delivers 250 brush strokes simultaneously to all teeth, saving 21 hours per year
2. **Built-In Oral Scanner:** AI-powered camera scans teeth, gums, tongue, and soft tissue for real-time health analysis and trend tracking
3. **AI Health Monitoring:** Feno Plus app provides personalized insights, links oral health to systemic disease risk (heart disease, diabetes), and offers early detection of inflammation/decay
4. **TrueFit Mouthpiece:** Personalized sizing based on individual dental arch for optimal fit and cleaning coverage
5. **99% Plaque Removal:** Near-complete plaque removal in 3 days (per internal studies) through comprehensive bristle coverage and ArcFlow Tech

### Product Design
- **Design elements:** Sleek, modern smarthandle with magnetic wireless charging base; U-shaped mouthpiece with precision-arranged nylon bristles; minimal controls (one-button operation)
- **Materials:** Medical-grade nylon bristles, food-safe silicone mouthpiece, premium plastic handle with turquoise accent details
- **Aesthetics:** Clean, minimalist industrial design with tech-forward aesthetic (Apple meets healthcare); turquoise brand accent color on charging base; professional, clinical appearance that signals premium quality

## Market Opportunity

### Market Size
- Global electric toothbrush market valued at $3.6 billion in 2023, projected to reach $6.5 billion by 2030 (CAGR 8.7%)
- Smart toothbrush segment growing faster at 10-12% annually as consumers adopt connected health devices
- Oral health monitoring market emerging as subset of $200+ billion preventative health industry

### Target Market Demographics
- 100+ million health-conscious adults in US alone (25-55 age range)
- $75k+ household income segment represents 40% of US households (52 million)
- Early health tech adopters: 35% of adults track fitness/health metrics (87 million people)
- Dental anxiety market: 36% of Americans fear dental visits, seeking at-home solutions

### Related Sub-Markets Growth
- Digital health monitoring devices growing 15-20% annually (smartwatches, CGMs, smart scales)
- Preventative healthcare services expanding as consumers shift from reactive to proactive health
- Oral probiotics and microbiome testing gaining traction (12% annual growth)

### Market Position
Feno positions itself as a **health platform first, toothbrush second**, competing less on cleaning performance and more on preventative health value. This positions Feno in the premium tier of oral care while creating a new product category that bridges consumer health tech and dental care. Key differentiator: only toothbrush that helps prevent systemic diseases through continuous monitoring.

## Why Now?
- **Rising health awareness post-pandemic:** Consumers increasingly invest in preventative health and at-home monitoring tools after COVID-19 heightened health consciousness
- **Oral-systemic health connection going mainstream:** Medical research linking gum disease to heart disease, diabetes, and cancer is now widely publicized, creating urgency for better oral care
- **Quantified-self movement:** Consumers already tracking sleep, fitness, nutrition are ready to add oral health monitoring to their data ecosystem
- **Time scarcity epidemic:** Busy professionals and parents seek efficiency without compromising health outcomes; 20-second solution addresses modern time constraints
- **AI and health tech maturity:** Consumer comfort with AI health insights (from Apple Watch, Oura Ring, etc.) creates receptive market for AI-powered oral scanning
